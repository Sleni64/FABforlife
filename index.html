PROJECT TEA

⭐️⭐️⭐️⭐️ 8/10 Big Booms for Project Tea – Almost Perfect! Project Tea in Chico is a go-to spot for a refreshing drink and a fun snack. The vibe is super chill, the drinks are creative and tasty (big fan of the boba options!), and the service is usually friendly and quick. They’ve earned 8 Big Booms from me 💥💥💥💥💥💥💥💥—because they almost have it all. The only thing holding them back from a perfect 10 is consistency with the Korean corn dogs. Sometimes they hit just right—crispy, cheesy, golden perfection—and other times, they’re a little underwhelming or not available at all. If they can tighten up the corn dog game, this place would be a 10/10 no question. Still, highly recommend stopping by when you’re in the area!

Mondos Coffeehouse

💥💥💥💥💥💥💥💥💥💥 / 10 Big Booms for Mondo’s Coffee – Pure Chico Magic Mondo’s Coffee? 10 BIG BOOMS, no question. This place is everything a coffee shop should be—cozy, welcoming, and packed with heart. The coffee is consistently top-tier (shoutout to the cold brew), and the bagel breakfast sandwiches? Unreal. Jalapeño cheddar, loaded eggy goodness, melty cheese—it’s the breakfast hug you didn’t know you needed. But what really sets Mondo’s apart is the vibe. It's not just about coffee—it's about community. Everyone is friendly, the space feels like a second home, and the mission behind it (supporting people with developmental disabilities) makes supporting them feel even better. Whether you're pulling up to study, hang with a friend, or just grab a quick bite, Mondo’s always delivers. 10/10 Big Booms. Mondo’s is the moment. 💥

The boba stop- Cozy, welcoming, grab & go If you’re in Chico and craving something refreshing, flavorful, and just a little fun, The Boba Stop should definitely be on your radar. Nestled just a few minutes from the Chico State campus, this small but vibrant shop delivers big on taste, creativity, and charm. Whether you’re a boba enthusiast or just looking for a sweet afternoon pick-me-up, this spot checks all the boxes. The drink menu at The Boba Stop is surprisingly diverse for such a small shop, offering everything from traditional milk teas to inventive snow bubbles and matcha blends. I started off with their Tiger Milk Tea, which features creamy milk tea layered with rich brown sugar syrup and chewy, fresh boba pearls. It’s sweet—but not overpoweringly so—with a smooth black tea base that brings balance. It’s the kind of drink that reminds you why boba took off in the first place.
